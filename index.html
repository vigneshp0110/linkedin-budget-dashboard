Campaign Architecture Decision — Vertical vs Theme-based

✅ Chosen: Vertical / Industry Structure
Software · Manufacturing · Financial Services · Other Industries
  • ✓ Zero audience overlap → no LinkedIn auction self-competition
  • ✓ Clear budget accountability per industry vertical
  • ✓ Theme creatives can still be rotated within each vertical campaign — achieving both theme-level and industry-level personalisation without structural complexity
  • ✓ Clean A/B testing and pipeline attribution per vertical
  • ✓ Easy CPM benchmarking — compare performance across verticals with clean, non-overlapping audiences
  • ✓ LinkedIn best practice for enterprise B2B campaigns
✗ Alternative: Theme-based Structure (not chosen)
Spreadsheet replacement · Mid-year comp cycle · Annual planning · etc.
  • ✗ Same company appears in multiple theme campaigns → LinkedIn self-competition → +15–30% CPM inflation
  • ✗ Themes overlap across personas — every Finance contact is both a spreadsheet user and a mid-year comp planner
  • ✗ Harder to budget bottom-up — theme audience sizes are fluid and overlap, making reliable spend forecasting difficult
↳

How We Solve for Theme-level Personalisation

Themes are a messaging and creative layer, not a structural one. The campaign architecture is built around audience segments (vertical × persona × seniority). Themes are layered on top through creative rotation inside each ad set — no new campaigns, no audience fragmentation, no CPM inflation.

EVERGREEN
Spreadsheet Replacement
Always-on. Umbrella theme that covers all sub-angles — rep experience, commission visibility, planning accuracy, payout errors. Runs year-round in all Finance and Ops persona ad sets across every vertical. This is the baseline creative layer the campaign never turns off.
SEASONAL
Mid-Year Comp Cycle
Time-bound. Runs April – July when H1 closes, comp reviews are live, and sales reps are questioning their numbers. Targets Finance and Sales persona ad sets. Layered on top of spreadsheet creatives during the season — does not replace them.
Delivery Mechanism — Rotate Evenly

LinkedIn's default delivery optimises toward the best-performing creative by CTR — which would progressively suppress mid-year comp creatives outside their seasonal peak and distort performance reads. Instead, set all ad sets to Rotate Evenly. This distributes impressions equally across all active creatives, giving each theme proportional airtime regardless of early CTR signals.

Non-Season (Aug – Mar)
Spreadsheet creatives only. Full 12× frequency behind a single message. LinkedIn has one narrative to reinforce — maximum depth.
Mid-Year Season (Apr – Jul)
Spreadsheet + Mid-Year Comp creatives active. Each theme gets ~6× frequency. Neither campaign is compromised — both narratives run in parallel to the same audience.
Managing the Frequency Split

Running two themes in parallel splits the 12× monthly frequency to ~6× per theme. If 6× feels thin during the mid-year peak — when the message has strong temporal urgency — the lever is a temporary budget increase to restore effective frequency. No structural changes. Scale back to baseline from August onward when spreadsheet runs alone again.

What Not To Do

Do not create separate ad sets per theme within the same campaign. Two Finance × Leadership ad sets targeting identical audiences within the same campaign will compete against each other in LinkedIn's internal auction — recreating the self-competition problem the vertical structure was designed to prevent, just moved inside the campaign instead of between campaigns.

The rule: Industry = campaign. Persona = ad set. Theme = creative. This gives a 3-axis personalisation matrix with zero structural overhead — the entire theme layer is managed through creative trafficking alone.

Bound Campaigns — Why a Defined TAM Deserves Its Own Campaign

Two campaigns — Xactly Displacement and AI Native — operate on a closed, pre-defined company list rather than the open cold TAM. They are excluded from cold campaigns entirely and run as standalone campaigns with their own budget, creative, and parameters.

Why exclude from cold?  Mixing these accounts into cold vertical campaigns forces creative compromise — the ad that works for a Xactly customer or an AI-native startup is fundamentally different from what works for a cold Software or Manufacturing prospect. Separate campaigns preserve creative intent, allow dedicated budget control, and prevent high-value accounts from being under-served by generic messaging.

Drop Sales Leadership persona from 4 cold verticals
− $34,453 / moBudget after: $178,272
What changed

Removed all T1 Sales Leadership ad sets (both Leadership and Champions seniority) from Software, Manufacturing, Financial Services, and Other Industries cold campaigns.

✦ Why this is defensible

Sales Leadership contacts are not left uncovered — they receive dedicated, higher-intent exposure via the Xactly Displacement campaign and Signal campaigns triggered by active hiring activity. Cold vertical Sales Leadership ad sets were creating LinkedIn auction self-competition, inflating CPMs on contacts these specialist campaigns already reach.

Drop all T2 ad sets
− $23,675 / moBudget after: $154,597
What changed

Removed all 22 T2-tier ad sets across Software, Manufacturing, Financial Services, and Other Industries. T2 accounts (10,284 companies) are smaller organisations typically below the ACV sweet spot for a complex ICM deployment.

✦ Why this is defensible
78% of cold TAM by company count still covered

After removing T2, we still serve 38,283 T1 companies — 78% of the addressable cold universe. T1 contacts are denser (avg 13.8 contacts/company vs T2's 9.7), stronger ICM processes, higher ACV ceiling. T2 can be reactivated once T1 pipeline matures.

LinkedIn Member Skills filter (AND condition) on top 3 Finance T1 ad sets
− $56,421 / moBudget after: $98,176
What changed

Added Member Skills (Sales Compensation, Commission Management, Incentive Compensation, Variable Pay) as AND conditions to Other Industries Finance Champions (90,427→27,128), Manufacturing Finance Champions (55,390→16,617), and Other Industries Finance Leadership (26,405→7,922). ~30% audience retention.

This is a relevance filter, not random truncation. Contacts with these skills are the actual Incentive Comp owners and evaluators. Reaching 27k skills-verified contacts is more valuable than blasting 90k generic Finance titles. Expected CPL improvement offsets volume reduction.

4
Signal campaigns: frequency 12× → 8× per month
− $2,178 / moBudget after: $95,998
What changed

Reduced delivery frequency from 12× to 8× per month across all 4 Signal campaign ad sets (Sales Headcount Growth, Sales Comp / ICM Hiring, Sales Leadership Hiring, GTM / RevOps Hiring).

✦ Why this is defensible

Signal campaigns are overlays — the same contacts simultaneously receive cold campaign impressions. At 12× signal + ~10× cold, combined exposure was ~22×/month — past fatigue threshold. Reducing signal to 8× brings combined exposure to ~18× while preserving signal-triggered creative context. 8× is still above the 4× minimum for aided recall lift.

5
Audience penetration: 60% → 50% across all non-signal ad sets
− $13,938 / moBudget after: $82,060
What changed

Reduced modelled monthly audience penetration from 60% to 50% across all cold, bound, and intent ad sets. Penetration represents the share of the target contact pool LinkedIn's algorithm is expected to reach in a given month.

✦ Why this is defensible

60% penetration was optimistic for large fixed audiences where LinkedIn's algorithm naturally caps reach before fatigue. At 50% penetration × 10× frequency, effective impression volume is 500 per 1,000 contacts monthly — above LinkedIn's recommended 400 for B2B brand consideration. A realism correction, not a strategic retreat.

6
Frequency: 12× → 10× per month across all non-signal ad sets
− $11,526 / moBudget after: $70,534
What changed

Reduced monthly delivery frequency from 12× to 10× across all cold, bound, and intent ad sets. Signal campaigns are separately set to 8× (Cut 4). The 12× baseline in Model 1 was an aggressive share-of-voice target; 10× reflects a sustainable steady-state.

✦ Why 10× is the right number
~5× per-creative frequency with Rotate Evenly delivery

With two creative themes rotating via LinkedIn's Rotate Evenly setting (Spreadsheet — always-on evergreen, Mid-Year Comp — seasonal Apr–Jul), effective per-creative frequency is ~5×/month — LinkedIn's recommended range for B2B awareness. Each impression feels fresh because the creative rotates. Diminishing recall returns beyond 10×/month for fixed cold audiences.
